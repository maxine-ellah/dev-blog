Time and Habits

14 January 2016

Which timebox and habits techniques did you try?

I set my timer to one hour and focused solely on the task for that time and tried to stay away from my phone.

What did you find interesting?

I related alot to the idea of 'The resistance' and not wanting to take risks in my work and the fear of making a mistake or 'standing out' and trying to silence those thoughts. I also liked the idea of creating little habits to change your behaviour permanently. It reminded me of the idea of neuroplasticity which I read a book on ages ago, and I like thinking that we are completely capable of changing the way we think and learning new things throughout our lives.

What worked for you?

Knowing my limits for staying focused on one thing is definitely an important factor for me. Although I timeboxed for and hour, I found it quite challenging to stay focused for that long, so next time I think I will try timeboxing for 30-45 minutes at a time. Taking breaks is also very important!

which will you experiment further with?

I'd like to pay more attention to how long I tend to spend on certain tasks, so I can more accurately timebox my time. making little habits based on already established behaviours is something I tried a few months ago and it worked quite well, so I will try and incorporate that into my work too.
